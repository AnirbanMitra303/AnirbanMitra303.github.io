Interested to know more about me? Here you go...

I (he/him/his) am currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Statistics, University of Pittsburgh. I am also the secretary of ASA Pittsburgh Student Chapter for 2020-21. Prior to that I used to live in Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh, India, while I was getting my Masters degree. I originally am from the outskirts of Kolkata, West Bengal, India. I came to the United States in August, 2017. Fun fact, I spent 10 years of my student life in hostels!

Ramakrishna Mission Vidyalaya, Narendrapur is my school, where I spent 8 years of my life. It indeed is a lovely place; me and my friends call it our second home! It being a residential school has hostels for all the students, playgrounds for everyone of them and ample greenery. I encourage you to find out more about my school here . Trust me, you would be surprised by the kind of life kids in my school have.

Even though I am pretty much immersed in Statistics all day, I have other interests too. I am an amateur photographer and mostly click landscapes. I also like to draw a few scratches on my iPad at times. I have always been interested in music and used to be in the school singing team. Early 2020, I finally got the opportunity and started learning the Guitar. If I had to pick my favorite instrument it would either be flute or sitar. Pt. Hariprasad Chaurasia and Pt. Ravi Shankar are my favorites and their music bring me peace. Recently, I have grown a massive interest in studying the night sky; so much so that it has thrown my sleeping cycle of the track! But this one merges quite nicely with my interest in photgraphy and I hope to get into astrophotography in the not so distant future. You may soon find me in the Astrostatistics Interest Group of the American Statistical Association.
